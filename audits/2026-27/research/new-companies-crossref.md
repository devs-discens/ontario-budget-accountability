### Arch Corporation

**Budget reference:** $133.6M Building Ontario Fund financing for 4 long-term care homes, 576 beds (p.100).

**Lobbyist registrations: None.** (Initial search returned "Monarch Corporation" — false positive from partial string match on "Arch" within "Monarch." Confirmed no results for "Arch Corporation" or its subsidiary "Riverdale Living.")

**About Arch:** Canadian private equity real estate firm, CEO Daniel Argiros. Operates 11 LTC homes in Ontario via Riverdale Living subsidiary. Portfolio spans 42,000 rental units across 30+ cities. Also connected to Conundrum Capital Corporation. ([archcorporation.com](https://archcorporation.com/about-us/))

**Elections Ontario:** Daniel Argiros donated $12,589 to the Progressive Conservative Party (2021-2024). Previously donated $3,881 to Liberals (2014). Total donations $16,470.

**BOF deal structure:** $265.5M total financing split evenly between BOF and Manulife. Four homes in Amherstburg, Lancaster, Prescott, and Tay Valley Township. ([Building Ontario Fund](https://buildingonfund.ca/building-ontario-fund-invests-in-four-rural-long-term-care-homes/))

**Assessment:** No registered lobbyists, but the CEO is a PC donor who received $133.6M in crown agency financing. The deal was co-financed with Manulife and appears to serve genuine infrastructure need (rural LTC). The BOF procurement process for this deal should be examined — was it competitive?

### Key Findings

1. **Kinross Gold's $5B Great Bear Project is lobbied by Rubicon Strategy** — the same firm run by Ford's campaign manager Kory Teneycke. The active lobbyist (Jan O'Driscoll) is a former Ford Cabinet chief of staff who was appointed Interim LCBO CEO in February 2026 — the same month Kinross received 1P1P fast-track designation. O'Driscoll donated $4,435 to the PC Party.

2. **Canada Nickel's $5B Crawford Project is lobbied by Sussex Strategy** — the same firm that lobbies for Aecon and Bruce Power (both already in our dataset). The active lobbyist (Mark Olsheski) is a former Queen's Park staffer who donated $15,407 to the PC Party. Crawford received 1P1P fast-track in January 2026.

3. **Both One Project One Process fast-tracked mining projects have active lobbyists from firms with documented ties to the Ford government.** The 1P1P framework was announced in October 2025. As of the 2026 Budget, three projects have been accepted: Frontier Lithium (no lobbyist found), Canada Nickel (Sussex Strategy), and Kinross Gold (Rubicon Strategy).

4. **Arch Corporation (LTC) has no lobbyists but its CEO donated $12,589 to the PC Party** and received $133.6M in BOF financing. This warrants monitoring given the history of LTC operators and political connections documented in the 2025-26 audit.

5. **8 of 12 new companies have no detectable political connections** in the Ontario lobbyist registry or provincial donation records. This doesn't rule out connections — it means they aren't visible in these datasets. Federal lobbying records, corporate board membership, and industry association ties could reveal additional relationships.
