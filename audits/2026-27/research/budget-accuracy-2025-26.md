# Did the Government Hit Its 2025-26 Targets?

*Comparing the 2025 Budget projections for fiscal year 2025-26 against the 2026 Budget interim actuals.*

**Source:** Ontario 2025 Budget (March 2025), Ontario 2026 Budget Table 3.1-3.4 (March 2026)

## Headline

The government projected a $14.6B deficit for 2025-26. The actual deficit was $12.3B — an improvement of $2.3B. But this wasn't spending discipline. Revenue came in $6.7B higher than projected. Spending also overran by $5.9B. The improvement is just the gap between two misses.

## Revenue: Projected $219.9B → Actual $226.6B (+$6.7B)

| Source | 2025 Budget Projection | 2026 Interim Actual | Difference |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|------------|
| Personal Income Tax | $57.8B | $61.9B | **+$4.1B** |
| Sales Tax | $40.1B | $39.2B | -$0.9B |
| Corporations Tax | $27.0B | $27.6B | +$0.6B |
| Federal Transfers | $38.8B | $39.1B | +$0.3B |
| GBE Income | $7.3B | $7.3B | — |
| All Other | $49.0B | $51.5B | +$2.5B |
| **Total Revenue** | **$219.9B** | **$226.6B** | **+$6.7B** |

**What drove it:** Personal Income Tax overperformed by $4.1B due to stronger-than-expected employment, equity market performance (capital gains and dividend income), and processing of prior year returns. Sales Tax underperformed by $0.9B, reflecting lower federal entitlement estimates. Corporation Tax came in $0.6B higher from better corporate profits.

**Note on comparability:** The 2026 Budget restated some revenue line items. Beginning with the 2025 Budget, Interest and Other Debt Servicing Charges (IOD) investment income is reported as part of revenue rather than netted against expense. The 2025 Budget total of $219,887M (Table 3.3) is on a comparable basis.

## Expense: Projected $232.5B → Actual $238.4B (+$5.9B)

| Sector | 2025 Budget Projection | 2026 Interim Actual | Difference |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|------------|
| Health | — | +$5,658M | **Largest overrun** |
| Children, Community and Social Services | — | +$1,100M | Social assistance demand |
| Justice | — | +$582M | Crown Liability Act, corrections |
| Education | — | -$462M | Lower student enrolment |
| Postsecondary Education | — | -$385M | International student cap |
| Other Programs | — | -$4,036M | Infrastructure underspend, Contingency drawdown |
| **Total Program Expense Change** | — | **+$2,458M** | — |
| Interest and Debt Servicing | — | -$130M | Efficient debt management |
| **Total Expense Change** | — | **+$5,938M** | — |

*Source: Table 3.4, changes since the 2025-26 Third Quarter Finances*

**Total Programs:** $216.3B projected → $222.4B actual (+$6.1B)
**Debt Servicing:** $16.2B projected → $16.0B actual (-$0.2B)
**Total Expense:** $232.5B projected → $238.4B actual (+$5.9B)

**What drove it:** Health sector spending overran by $5.7B — the single largest miss. This was driven by utilization-based programs (OHIP, Ontario Public Drug Programs) growing faster than forecast. Social services overran by $1.1B from higher-than-expected demand for social assistance. Justice overran by $582M, mainly from obligations under the *Crown Liability and Proceedings Act, 2019*. These overruns were partially offset by $4.0B in underspending on infrastructure programs and drawdown of the Contingency Fund.

## Deficit: Projected ($14.6B) → Actual ($12.3B) — improved $2.3B

| | 2025 Budget | 2026 Interim | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $219.9B | $226.6B | +$6.7B |
| Expense | $232.5B | $238.4B | +$5.9B |
| Surplus/(Deficit) Before Reserve | ($12.6B) | ($11.8B) | +$0.8B |
| Reserve | $2.0B | $0.5B | -$1.5B |
| **Surplus/(Deficit)** | **($14.6B)** | **($12.3B)** | **+$2.3B** |

The $2.0B reserve was drawn down to $0.5B. Without the reserve drawdown, the improvement was only $0.8B.

## Borrowing: Projected $42.8B → Actual $58.6B (+$15.8B)

This is the most significant deviation. Long-term borrowing for 2025-26 was $58.6B — $15.8 billion higher than the $42.8B in the 2025 Budget. The government explains this as "pre-funding" 2026-27 borrowing requirements and the $5B Protect Ontario Account. But it means Ontario borrowed $15.8B more than planned in a single fiscal year.

## Net Debt: Projected 37.8% of GDP → Actual 36.8% of GDP

Net debt-to-GDP came in better than projected, at 36.8% vs the 2025 Budget's 37.8%. This reflects the stronger GDP denominator (Ontario's nominal GDP came in higher than forecast) rather than lower actual debt.

## Assessment

| Metric | Hit Target? | Notes |
|--------|------------|-------|
| Revenue | Beat by $6.7B | PIT windfall from employment + equity markets |
| Program Expense | Missed by $6.1B | Health overran by $5.7B alone |
| Debt Servicing | Beat by $0.2B | Lower interest costs |
| Deficit | Beat by $2.3B | Revenue windfall > expense overrun |
| Borrowing | Missed by $15.8B | Pre-funded next year + Protect Ontario Account |
| Net Debt/GDP | Beat by 1.0pp | Higher GDP, not lower debt |

**Bottom line:** The government beat its deficit target, but only because revenue came in hot — not because spending was controlled. Program spending overran by $6.1B. The $2.3B deficit improvement masks a $5.7B health spending overrun, $1.1B in social services pressure, and $15.8B in unplanned borrowing. The reserve was nearly exhausted ($2.0B → $0.5B).
