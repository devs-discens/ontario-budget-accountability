# Ontario Budget Accountability Project

An open-source accountability audit of the Ontario provincial budget. Traces public money from revenue sources through spending decisions to final recipients, and documents the political connections between budget beneficiaries and government insiders.

## What this is

This is **original research**, structured as both human-readable reports and machine-readable data. It covers:

- **Two budget years:** 2025-26 ($232.5B) and 2026-27 ($244.2B)
- **80 named spending decisions** with contract values, decision types, and recipients
- **42 political figures** — politicians, lobbyists, government insiders, and executives
- **101 documented relationships** between people, companies, and government — each with source URLs
- **13 policy actions** (legislation, MZOs, exemptions) that create spending or benefit specific parties
- **12 government board appointments** to crown agencies and public bodies
- **4 cross-referenced public datasets:** Elections Ontario (429K contributions), Public Accounts (30K payments), Sunshine List (2.4M+ salary records), Ontario Lobbyist Registry (390 registrations)

### Key findings

- 31 of 80 tracked spending decisions (39%) involve companies with documented insider political connections
- $80.7B flows through insider-connected companies
- $67.9B in sole-source contracts (41% of tracked value) awarded without competition
- 18 decisions flagged by the Auditor General
- 13 former Ford government staff became lobbyists for companies receiving public money — 2 found to have broken lobbying rules
- 3 active investigations: RCMP (Greenbelt), OPP (Skills Development Fund), Integrity Commissioner (Minister Piccini)

### 15 notable facts

1. **Mike Harris** earned ~$3.5M + ~$6-7M in stock as Chartwell Board Chair (2003-2022) — after privatizing long-term care as Premier
2. For-profit LTC homes had **96% more COVID cases and 78% more deaths** than non-profits ([CMAJ peer-reviewed study](https://www.cmaj.ca/content/192/33/E946))
3. The Ford government gave LTC operators **retroactive legal immunity** (Bill 218) while Ford insiders lobbied for them during the pandemic
4. **80% of for-profit Skills Development Fund** money went to PC donor-led companies ([The Trillium](https://www.thetrillium.ca/news/the-trillium-investigations/labour-minister-has-given-pc-donors-companies-80-of-for-profit-businesses-skills-development-funds-11746974))
5. **35 SDF recipients** made their first-ever PC donation *after* the fund was created
6. Therme (Ontario Place spa) **claimed to operate 6 spas — NYT found 5 were not theirs**. Infrastructure Ontario did not verify.
7. Ontario Place total cost rose from **$335-424M to $2.237B** (Auditor General, December 2024)
8. Mark Saunders was paid up to **$171K/year** as Ontario Place "Special Advisor." FOI found **zero records of any work product.**
9. **Mark Lawson** went from Ford's Deputy Chief of Staff → VP at Therme Group → now lobbies for Billy Bishop terminal owner. His wife is Chief of Staff for Cabinet Office.
10. **Highway 413:** government refuses to disclose cost. 8 developers owning land along the route donated **$753K to PCs + $60K to Ontario Proud**
11. Greenbelt removal: AG estimated **$8B windfall** for De Gasperis family. **RCMP criminal investigation ongoing.**
12. The Mississauga Hospital ($13.9B) received **only one bid**. AG found it **~$4B over** originally approved budget.
13. **Nico Fidani-Diker** (Ford's former Executive Assistant): **5 separate integrity violations** including Greenbelt lobbying and offering Maple Leafs tickets to officials
14. Ontario Science Centre: draft engineering reports recommended routine maintenance — government **closed it anyway**. Architect called it "a political move."
15. AtkinsRealis **hired the sitting PC Energy Minister** as VP after he oversaw nuclear policy — then received $2.1B+ in contracts

## How to use this

### Read the research
Start with the reports in `audits/2025-26/research/`. They contain the narrative findings with ~200 source URLs.

- [Spending Report](audits/2025-26/research/spending-report.md) — Where $232.5B goes, company by company
- [Political Connections](audits/2025-26/research/political-connections.md) — Who knows whom and how they got the contracts
- [Cross-Reference Report](audits/2025-26/research/cross-reference-report.md) — Unified view joining Elections Ontario, Public Accounts, Sunshine List, and Lobbyist Registry data against all 129 tracked organizations and 42 people
- [Budget Accuracy Check](audits/2026-27/research/budget-accuracy-2025-26.md) — 2025 Budget projections vs 2025-26 interim actuals
- [Year-over-Year Diff](audits/2026-27/research/budget-diff-2025-vs-2027.md) — 2024-25 actual vs 2026-27 plan

### Explore the data
The structured JSON in `audits/2025-26/data/` is designed for analysis. Each file has source URLs on every claim.

### View the dashboard (optional)
```bash
cd dashboard
python3 -m http.server 8765
# Open http://localhost:8765
```

### Import a new budget year
```bash
cd dashboard
python3 scripts/import_budget.py template 2026-27    # Create template
# Fill in numbers from the new budget
python3 scripts/import_budget.py diff 2025-26 2026-27  # Compute changes
python3 scripts/import_budget.py validate 2026-27      # Check integrity
```

## How to contribute

This project benefits from more eyes. You can help by:

1. **Verifying claims** — Pick any entry in the data files, check the source URL, confirm the facts. File an issue if something is wrong.
2. **Adding connections** — If you find a documented political connection we've missed (with a source URL), submit a PR.
3. **Updating for new developments** — New AG reports, Integrity Commissioner findings, contract awards, or news investigations.
4. **Auditing a new budget year** — The 2026-27 budget analysis is underway in `audits/2026-27/`. Follow the [methodology](methodology/README.md) to extend it or audit future years.
5. **Forking for other jurisdictions** — The [schema](methodology/schema.md) is designed to work for any Canadian province. Fork it for Alberta, BC, Quebec, etc.

### Standards for contributions

- Every factual claim must have a `source_url` pointing to a primary source or credible journalism
- Political connections must be documented facts, not inferences
- Connection strength ratings must follow the [verification guide](methodology/verification.md)
- No unsourced claims about individuals — this is accountability, not accusation

## Limitations

- Ontario does not publish a consolidated vendor-by-payment-amount list
- Hospital-level operating funding allocations are not public (confirmed by FAO)
- The Ontario Lobbyist Registry is less searchable than the federal registry
- Big 4 consulting spend at the provincial level is not aggregated
- Highway 413 total cost has never been officially disclosed
- Some contract values represent the total consortium value, not individual company shares — this is noted in the data
- Political donations data is limited to what's available through Elections Ontario public filings
- Lobbyist Registry results are capped at 10 per search (first results page); 13 orgs hit this cap and likely have more registrations
- Sunshine List data covers 1996-2020 + 2025; years 2021-2024 not yet downloaded
- Public Accounts covers direct provincial payments only — crown agency disbursements (Metrolinx, IO, OPG) are excluded, which is why 42 of 129 tracked organizations show no payment match despite receiving government contracts

## Data collection

The `scripts/` directory contains tools for downloading and cross-referencing four public data sources against the 129 organizations and 42 people tracked in this audit. All scripts use Python standard library only (no pip dependencies).

```bash
# Elections Ontario: download bulk contributions and cross-reference
python3 scripts/cross_reference_donations.py --mode download
python3 scripts/cross_reference_donations.py --mode crossref

# Public Accounts: cross-reference payment data
python3 scripts/cross_reference_payments.py

# Sunshine List: download salary data and cross-reference
python3 scripts/cross_reference_sunshine.py --mode download
python3 scripts/cross_reference_sunshine.py --mode crossref

# Lobbyist Registry: scrape and search (polite: 2.5s between requests)
python3 scripts/scrape_lobbyist_registry.py --mode discover          # inspect form structure
python3 scripts/scrape_lobbyist_registry.py --mode organizations     # search all 129 orgs
python3 scripts/scrape_lobbyist_registry.py --mode people            # search all tracked lobbyists
python3 scripts/scrape_lobbyist_registry.py --mode targets           # search by govt officials
python3 scripts/scrape_lobbyist_registry.py --mode single --query "Ontario Shipyards" --field client
```
